    The Greek Tetrad Method

    The Greek Tetrad was a 4-day training cycle used by ancient Greek athletes preparing for the Olympic Games, documented by philosopher Philostratus. Each day served a distinct physiological purpose. This program adapts that ancient wisdom into a modern performance framework:

    Day 1 — Proparaskeue (Prep & Plyo): The preparation. Plyometric training primes the nervous system, activates fast-twitch muscle fibers, and builds explosive power. Sprint intervals and reactive jumps forge athletic readiness for the heavy work to come.

    Day 2 — Anabasis (Heavy Strength): The ascent. Maximum load on the compound lifts — squat, deadlift, press, pull. The body is driven to its structural limits. This is where raw strength is built.

    Day 3 — Katharsis (Mobility & Yoga): The purification. Active recovery through yoga flows, joint mobility work, and mindful walking. The body is cleansed of fatigue, connective tissue is restored, and the nervous system is reset for peak performance.

    Day 4 — Techne (Skills & Mastery): The craft. Deliberate skill practice — gymnastics, Olympic lift technique, sport-specific work. High neurological quality at managed intensity. Mastery is built through precision, not pressure.
